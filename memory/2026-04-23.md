### Key Findings
- 5 indicators: GDP/capita PPP (30%), GNI/capita (20%), HDI (20%), Gini (15%), relative poverty rate (15%)
- Min-max normalization within panels, inverted for Gini and poverty
- 31 countries in global panel, plus regional panels (Africa, Latin America, Asia)
- Removed savings rate in 2026 edition (favored rentier states like Qatar)
- Honest about limitations: editorial weights, outdated data for some countries, estimated poverty rates
- 7 alternative weighting configs tested — top 5 stable

### Critical Takeaway for Democracy Pulse
- HelloSafe is fundamentally an economic index despite "prosperity" branding
- 60-70% effective weight on income-related measures (HDI includes GNI)
- Zero governance, rights, freedom, or democratic quality indicators
- Singapore 6th, Qatar 11th, UAE 13th — authoritarian states score high
- Validates our need for a political/governance dimension, but also validates going beyond GDP
- Their confidence flagging (⚠️ per country per indicator) is worth adopting
- Their robustness testing across weight configs is worth adopting
- Their data source hierarchy (Eurostat → WB → OECD) is worth formalizing for us too

### GDP-GNI Gap — First Extraction Analysis
- **Norway: -$19,404** — GNI exceeds GDP. Residents earn more from abroad than foreign capital extracts. Favorable extraction indicator. (Sovereign wealth fund income, shipping, offshore)
- **Turkey: +$7,040** — GDP far exceeds GNI. Significant foreign capital extraction. Domestic production exceeds what residents earn. Classic peripheral economy pattern.
- **USA: +$1,032** — Small gap. Modest net foreign capital presence. Much smaller than Ireland/Luxembourg distortions.
